THE PROGRAM

The CS Scholars Program at UC Berkeley offers a "home" within Cal for diverse undeclared Letters and Science students interested in the computer science major. We aim to support students from underserved backgrounds who might not have had access to resources that prepare students to succeed in computer science. We seek passionate, creative students who wish to give back to the community and to each other.

COLLABORATIVE PROBLEM-SOLVERS, INNOVATORS, and LEADERS

CS Scholars provides a cohesive environment where scholars commit to the success of one another. Scholars benefit from a host of academic, leadership and professional development opportunities. For example, scholars attend required entry level computer science courses (specifically, CS 61A and 61B), labs, discussion sections as a cohort. They attend a special seminar devoted to academic success and career development. Each Scholar works closely with the Associate Director for L&S CS Undergraduate Matters in order to prepare for admission to the UC Berkeley Letters and Science Computer Science major. Scholars also benefit from specialized tutoring, faculty mentoring, peer mentoring, a dedicated departmental study space and industry site visits.

SUCCESS

CS Scholars seeks to attract and retain more diverse students to a field that has long been challenged in terms of diversity. Because most of our members come from under-resourced high schools, economically disadvantaged backgrounds, and are the first in their families to attend college, that success may mean taking courses, doing research, and graduating on a "different clock" than their classmates. CS Scholars helps students to develop an individualized plan to get them to where they want to go.

Our first year is already showing promising results. Our first cohort had a 100% completion rate for CS 61A (compared to 87% for the class overall) and maintained a higher GPA overall than the general class.
